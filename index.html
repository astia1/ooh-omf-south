Project Overview

Sound Transit is building a new garage at Sumner Station so that more people can conveniently access regional transit and enjoy traffic-free travel.

Improvements for pedestrians and bicyclists have already been completed at the Sumner Station. This includes sidewalk and lighting enhancements in the station area and a new sidewalk and multi-use path on the Traffic Avenue/ SR 410 interchange. This work was completed by the City of Sumner with approximately $7.5 million in funding from Sound Transit.

Construction on the garage begins this summer 2023, and we expect it to open to the public in early 2026. Keep scrolling to view building designs and learn what to expect during construction.

Construction Impacts

 

Parking + 

Because the garage is being built where the Sound Transit surface parking is located today, parking will be reduced during construction. At least 110 stalls will be available at all times, including all 68 at the lot north of Maple Street.
There are several other options for getting to a Sounder Station during construction:
  • Walk or ride your bicycle to the station.
  • Carpool to the station. Carpools can get reserved parking – learn more here.
  • Park at Puyallup Station and park in one of the 673 new stalls in the garage and new surface lot.
  • Park at Bonney Lake Park-and-Ride Lot (356 stalls) and catch ST Express 596 to Sumner Station.

Parking policies

Will I have to pay to park at the garage? + 

Under current Sound Transit policy, monthly paid parking permits are offered at facilities that fill to capacity on a regular basis. If the Sumner garage fills up regularly after opening, we might consider starting a monthly parking permit program here.

Will there be electric vehicle charging ports? There will not be electric vehicle charging ports in the garage, but we are wiring the facility so that these could be added in the future. (?)

Can local residents park in the garage on weekends? Sound Transit parking facilities are intended for use by transit passengers. If you will be attending a special event by train on the weekend, you will be welcome to park in the garage.

Safety and security

What safety and security features are included in this garage?     + 

Some of the new safety features of the garage include:

  • Landscaping designed to ensure clear visibility of vehicles and pedestrians.
  • Ample lighting in the garage interior and exterior.
  • Visible stairwells and elevator shafts.
  • Security cameras throughout the garage, including elevators.
  • Emergency phones at every corner of each floor.
  • Random security and police patrols 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Sound Transit also has a security operations center that monitors activity across our facilities 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Traffic and parking impacts

Once the garage opens, will there be more traffic on my street? + 

Most traffic entering and leaving the station after the garage is built will be via Station Lane on the south end of the site.

Can I park on a neighborhood street near the station when I ride Sounder? + 

The City of Sumner has zoned most streets near the station as "RPZ-3" which means only residents with a residential parking pass are allowed to park for more than three hours. We recommend all passengers either: park at Bonney Lake Park and Ride; park at Puyallup Station; apply for an HOV parking permit; or walk or bicycle to the station.

Sounder trains

Why doesn’t Sounder run more often? + 

Sounder runs primarily on tracks owned by the BNSF Railroad, used heavily for freight movements. We do provide special event trains for select events, like some Seahawks, Sounders FC, and Mariners games. See the event service schedule. In addition, the ST3 ballot measure included funds for additional trips on Sounder South, which per Board policy would be implemented by 2046.

How do I know which track the train is arriving on? + 

Trains traveling northbound in the morning and southbound in the afternoon generally use the primary platform on the south side of the tracks. Morning southbound trains and afternoon northbound trains generally use the platform on the north side of the tracks. A station agent is on site at the platform when trains are running, and can direct passengers to the appropriate track, or notify them if there are last-minute track changes.

Will Sound Transit build a pedestrian bridge over the tracks?     + 

Construction of a pedestrian bridge over the train tracks is not part of this project, but Sound Transit may consider building a bridge in the future.

Background: Sounder South and Regional Bus Service

Schedule

Sounder South trains offer a dependable, traffic-free commute between Lakewood and Seattle. We currently operate nine round trips on weekdays. For details, read our schedule. Adult fares range from $3.75 to $5.75 one way, and fares for seniors, youth and low-income individuals are lower. Powered by clean-burning, ultra-low sulfur diesel, Sounder runs primarily on tracks owned by BNSF Railway. While Sound Transit owns the trains and stations and provides security, BNSF operates Sounder, and Amtrak provides maintenance. Sounder trains offer comfortable seats, surge-protected outlets for laptops and tablets, Wi-Fi service, storage for up to four bicycles per car, overhead storage, climate control and restrooms. Some cars have worktables.
